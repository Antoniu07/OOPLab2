CAESAR CIPHER FREQUENCY ANALYSIS - REPORT

1. METHODOLOGY
--------------
Tested all 3 metrics across 10 texts and 5 shifts (3,7,13,19,25) = 50 tests each.
Texts ranged from 10 to 230 letters, including edge cases.


2. RESULTS
----------

Text                         | Letters | Chi-Sq | Euclid | Cosine
-----------------------------|---------|--------|--------|-------
"hello world"                |   10    |  0/5   |  0/5   |  0/5
"attack at dawn"             |   12    |  5/5   |  0/5   |  0/5
"the quick brown fox"        |   16    |  0/5   |  5/5   |  5/5
"the quick brown fox jumps.."|   35    |  5/5   |  5/5   |  5/5
"to be or not to be..."      |   42    |  5/5   |  5/5   |  5/5
"in the beginning..."        |  115    |  5/5   |  5/5   |  5/5
"it was the best of times..."|  230    |  5/5   |  5/5   |  5/5
"aaaa bbbb cccc"             |   12    |  0/5   |  0/5   |  0/5
"buzz fizz jazz quiz fuzz"   |   20    |  0/5   |  0/5   |  0/5
"buffalo" x6                 |   42    |  0/5   |  0/5   |  0/5

Overall: Chi-Squared 50%, Euclidean 50%, Cosine 50%


3. KEY FINDINGS
---------------
- 35+ letters: all 3 metrics achieve 100% accuracy.
- Under 20 letters: unreliable. Chi-Squared won on "attack at dawn"
  (rich in common letters a,t), Euclidean/Cosine won on "the quick
  brown fox" (more evenly spread letters). Neither is consistently better.
- Edge cases (repetitive text, rare letters, limited vocabulary):
  all 3 metrics fail because the text doesn't resemble normal English.


4. CONCLUSION
-------------
Text length is the critical factor, not the choice of metric. All three
perform identically on 35+ letter natural English text. For short texts,
no metric is reliable. Chi-Squared is recommended as a slight favorite
due to its statistical normalization, but the difference is marginal.
